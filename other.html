Participatory Design Workshop

During the participatory design workshop, we observed participants planning a day in Edinburgh using a Fringe Festival map. They marked routes, discussed travel options, and shared their decision-making process.

Interview with Edinburgh Tourist Office

We conducted a short interview with the Edinburgh Tourist Office to gain insights into existing tools and challenges in managing tourist traffic during the Fringe Festival.

Insights:

  • All attractions & sites are all centrally located, clustered together.
  • Wide range of people use the iCentre.
  • They don't often tailor towards students.
  • STA closed after Covid.

Interview with Joshua Ryan-Saha

We interviewed Joshua Ryan-Saha who is one of the co-founders' of Traveltech, who provided valuable advice on creating a social and localized tool that stands out among existing tourist apps.

Insights: He gave us background on tourism and how people in general plan their activities. He told us that planning a trip involves 5 stages: dreaming, planning, booking, experiencing, and sharing. The advice was that we had to decide what part of the process our device would intervene with. We decided that our app was helpful for the planning and experiencing stages.

Our Team Solution: FringeEasy

Our solution, FringeEasy, addresses the overtourism problem in Edinburgh by combining interactive maps, personalized recommendations, and community-driven features. The goal is to encourage exploration of hidden gems while reducing congestion at popular tourist hotspots.

Map Ranking System: Highlights attractions based on popularity and type.

FringeEasy Map: Provides real-time data on tourist congestion and travel recommendations.

Key Features of FringeEasy

  • Encourage Community: Mitigates place alienation by promoting interaction between students and locals to discover attractions.
  • Travel Actively: Suggests walking and public transport routes for sustainable travel.
  • Champion Uniqueness: Highlights lesser-known attractions, fostering a sense of discovery and adventure.

What we offer: Available on the University of Edinburgh Student Association website, FringeEasy combines comprehensive trip planning tools with real-time tourist data to reduce reliance on multiple resources.

Follow-Up: Gamifying FringeEasy

As a follow-up to FringeEasy, I am working on gamifying the platform to encourage visitors to explore less popular and hidden attractions. The goal is to make the experience more interactive and rewarding for users while supporting sustainable tourism and reducing congestion at popular hotspots.

Key Gamification Features

  • Points System: Earn points for visiting lesser-known attractions, completing themed routes, or reviewing locations.
  • Achievement Badges: Unlock badges for exploring unique spots, such as ‚ÄúHidden Gem Explorer‚Äù or ‚ÄúLocal Culture Champion.‚Äù

Why Gamify?

Gamification not only adds a fun and engaging layer to FringeEasy but also serves to:

  • Encourage sustainable tourism by incentivizing exploration of underappreciated areas.
  • Promote community engagement by fostering a sense of achievement and friendly competition.
  • Support local businesses by driving traffic to less frequented locations.
