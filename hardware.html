CubeScrub

CubeScrub is a Cartesian robot designed to move along three axes (X, Y, Z) to effectively clean flat surfaces. Below is a detailed overview of its development, from concept to implementation.

Previous Work and Objectives

CubeScrub was inspired by existing Cartesian robot systems and automated cleaning devices. The goal was to design a modular, efficient, and user-friendly cleaning robot.

Design Iterations

Each design iteration brought new insights and challenges. Below are the iterations, along with their advantages and disadvantages for discussion.

Design Iteration 1

Advantages: Simplicity, lightweight design.

Disadvantages: Limited motion precision.

Design Iteration 2

Advantages: Improved stability, modular frame.

Disadvantages: Increased weight, higher material costs.

Prototyping Functional Units

CubeScrub's functional units were prototyped in stages, from initial sketches to hardware implementation. Below are some of the key units and their corresponding hardware prototypes.

Z-Axis: Responsible for vertical motion using a servo motor.

X-Y Axis: Utilizes a belt and pulley mechanism for smooth horizontal motion.

Spinning Arm: A flexible cleaning attachment powered by a servo motor.
