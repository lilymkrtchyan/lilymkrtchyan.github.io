Telegram LiveChat

A user-centric, real-time messaging feature designed to bring transparency and engagement to Telegram chats.

Telegram LiveChat: Case Study

Telegram LiveChat is a feature designed to enable real-time transparency and interaction in conversations by showing what the other user is typing in real time. It combines innovation with user research to create a feature that addresses the lack of personalization and transparency in traditional messaging apps.

User Research and Problem Analysis

During the research phase, we identified two primary challenges:

  • The lack of self-expression and personalization features in messaging apps.
  • The lack of transparency between users during conversations.

Brainstorming Solutions

Brainstorming solutions to enhance transparency and personalization in Telegram.

Feature Prioritization

To decide which solutions to prioritize, we used an impact-effort matrix to evaluate feasibility and potential impact. This process helped us focus on high-impact and feasible solutions, like LiveChat.

Emoji Moodboard: Mid-Fidelity Prototype

The Emoji Moodboard feature lets users share their current mood through emojis while chatting. Below is the mid-fidelity prototype showcasing how users can select emojis, view their mood history, and display their emoji mood in their profile.

SWOT Analysis

S W O T
Allows the user to express their feelings, emotions, and individuality. There are limited options of emojis that may not convey the whole range of the user’s feelings and emotions. Sometimes it is hard to tell how you feel through a text because you don’t know what to say, how to say, or what reaction to expect. Hence emoji is an easier way to convey feelings without taking the direct responsibility of talking to other people about your feelings. There are other mood tracking software applications that specialize at this.
Allows the user to track their mood and to look back at their mood record through a mood-board. Mood tracking is not the primary purpose of Telegram, however it can start a conversation. People are interested in data about themselves, hence they would use the feature and look at the emoji mood board. In the future, we might also create wraps (like Spotify does) and draw associations with all the factors and conversations that made the user happy or sad, and we can show them their mood and their notes from a year ago (like Google photos does). Emojis might replace human interaction and conversation.
As a user you can understand other user’s feelings before reaching out to them. Discourages direct communication and opens a room for misunderstanding and prejudice. As a user you can be conscious when you see someone is grieving and can be supportive. Alternatively, as a user you can wait until the user has a positive mood to talk to them to ensure the best possible conversation. Can take away the main purpose of the app.
Increases transparency between users. Users can support each other based on their displayed emojis and can start a conversation if they need a clarification or if they are curious.
Easy to use. Users tend to use the features that are easy to use. Choosing one emoji each day is fairly easy to use, hence the impact that it will leave on the user experience is immense.

Telegram LiveChat: Mid-Fidelity Prototype

The Telegram LiveChat mid-fidelity prototype demonstrates the real-time messaging feature, showcasing how users can view each other's typing in progress. This feature brings a new layer of transparency and engagement to conversations.

SWOT Analysis

S W O T
Has two modes: the usual mode and the live chat. Accommodates for both groups of people. Additionally, the feature works only when both (or all) members of the chat have it on (like the active feature). By leaving the usual mode as an option we are risking people not trying out the live chat as they are already used to the usual mode. Users are interested to see what the other user is typing for so long or deleting and never sending in the usual mode; hence, they will try out the new feature at least once. There are user groups that would never turn the feature on, such as people who overthink and edit their messages several times, people who send sensitive and important messages, or people who use Telegram as a platform for formal communication.
Opens up a space for new formats of communication. Users can live chat each other when having a debate or a discussion. Users can also live chat while brainstorming or interviewing. Users may send long messages and never save or “complete” them, having essays instead of a discussion. If the users are too concentrated on their own message, they would not read the other users’ messages. Text interviewing might become an option for people who have jobs related to typing, writing, and editing. Other types of interviews might request the user to write down their thoughts to see their flow of thinking. Users can prepare their “perfect” messages beforehand and input them from somewhere else as they type. This way they would not edit or change anything and they would not be honest and transparent.
Users can live chat each other instead of video-calling or audio-calling when they want to talk in real time but cannot speak or listen. If the users type fast, this will be no different than usual texting. Live chatting can allow photos to replace the experience of seeing each other in a video call. Users have to “complete” their real-time messages in order to move on and have a conversation, instead of two long messages.
Allows users to see each others’ flow of thoughts and to better understand each other. If the users are bad at messaging or writing in general, it can get confusing and irritating to watch the other user struggle in real time. As the live chat allows both users to see each other's messages (even incomplete and unfinished ones) and type simultaneously, the other user can guide and lead the conversation as opposed to sitting and waiting for a message in a regular mode. If users who communicate in foreign languages see making spelling and grammar mistakes as a threat and embarrassment, they would not use the feature and would be excluded.
Increases transparency. By watching each other type in real time, users will know exactly what the other user is thinking. Users’ spelling and grammatical mistakes will be immediately seen. Users won’t have the chance to double-check themselves or run their messages by checking websites. This is especially unfair to the users who do not communicate in their native language and have to check words and phrases in a foreign language. Users will have the chance to get to know the real users. Furthermore, authentic relationships that will form as a result of this feature will weigh more in the eyes of the users than grammar and spelling mistakes.

Why Live Chat is the Optimal Choice

In our journey to innovate Telegram's messaging experience, we evaluated three potential features — Emoji Moodboard, Telegram Stories, and Live Chat. After an in-depth analysis and prototyping process, we chose to proceed with Live Chat as the feature that most effectively addresses user needs and sets Telegram apart in the competitive messaging landscape.

Addressing User Pain Points

Texting-induced anxiety: Many users experience stress from typing indicators ("bubbles") and the ambiguity of waiting for a response or deleted messages.

Lack of engagement: Users desire a more dynamic and interactive way to communicate, resembling in-person interactions.

Differentiation from Competitors

Live Chat redefines messaging by enabling users to see what the other person is typing in real time, offering a unique communication experience that:

  • Alleviates texting-induced anxiety by showing the flow of thoughts during messaging.
  • Engages users in a completely new way.
  • Reinforces Telegram's identity as a platform that prioritizes innovation and authenticity.

Enhancing Transparency and Engagement

  • Reduce ambiguity: By revealing unfiltered thoughts and typing processes, users can better understand one another.
  • Foster authenticity: Real-time interactions create more genuine relationships.
  • Boost engagement: Users can respond dynamically during a conversation, as if they were face-to-face.

Future Potential of Live Chat

  • Integration with real-time collaboration tools for professional use.
  • Expansion into multimedia interactions, like real-time video or photo editing.
  • Analytics to provide insights into communication patterns.

High-Fidelity Prototyping: Live Chat Implementation

The Live Chat feature introduces innovative user flows to make real-time communication seamless and transparent. Below are the detailed flows for different scenarios, presented in a concise table format:

User Flow Description
Chats → A specific chat → The other user typing Observe another user typing in real time, showing their thought process and enhancing the transparency of conversations.
Chats → A specific chat → You, as the user, typing Your typing actions are visible, allowing the other user to see your thought process and reply in real time.
Chats → A specific chat → Both you and the other user typing Both users type simultaneously, creating an interactive experience akin to a real-time conversation.
Chats → Menu → Settings → Chat settings → Activate live chat Users can activate or deactivate Live Chat through the settings menu, offering flexibility based on individual preferences.
Chats → Menu → Create a new group → Add the group members → Chats → Different (all) users typing Supports group chats where multiple users engage in live typing simultaneously, fostering collaborative discussions.

Questions Considered

During the development of Telegram Live Chat, we carefully examined the following critical questions to ensure an intuitive and engaging user experience:

  • How would the other user’s typing look like?
  • How would my, as the user’s, typing look like?
  • How to indicate that the message is complete?
  • How to turn on and off the feature button would look like?

How Would Activating the Live Chat Look Like?

The route: Menu → Settings → Chat Settings → Live Chat (On)

The Live Chat activation switch would be within the settings as it is related to other settings of the chat within the information hierarchy. The user would switch the Live Chat switch from “off” condition to “On” condition. The switch looks and functions identical to all the other buttons related to similar settings within the information hierarchy, hence there should be no confusion as to how to use it.

How Does The Typing Box Look Like For Us?

VERSION 1:

In this version we, the user, type Ok as the other user is typing their message. This is a very unique function of this message, as the user — we — can see/reply to the other user’s message even if that message is yet incomplete. In this variation of typing we explore the visual appearance of our typing box. Namely, unlike the typing box of the other user, the typing box for us, as the user, is not changed from the original Telegram feature (when Live Chat is off).

VERSION 2:

In the second variation of the visual exploration of the typing box, we explore the typing box that looks identical to what the other user is seeing (typing in process).

THE CHOICE:

I think that the second version of the typing box is more suitable for the feature as it displays exactly how the user on the other side of the conversation would see the typing. It avoids the confusion that may occur with the traditional typing box and it shows the typing user exactly what the other person is seeing.

What Would Indicate That The Message Is Complete?

In the original Telegram messaging, there are two ticks, one indicating that the message is delivered, and the second one indicating that the message was read. This, however, would not work for the Live Chat as the user might read half of the message only (if they see the message in the process) and not see all of it. There also needs to be a clarification of when the message is complete so the user knows that the thought is complete and that they can reply to a complete message. For these two reasons, I have designed a message layout that lights up when the message is complete (when the user presses enter). The Enter key in the traditional Telegram messaging sends the message to the chat, however, in the Live Chat it completes the message.
